1. The Core Tradeoff & Mechanism

Given the strict ≤ 2.0x bandwidth budget and the immense latency penalty of ARQ retransmissions, we designed a custom UDP transport protocol utilizing Forward Error Correction (FEC). To remain under budget, we implemented a 2-frame XOR parity grouping. For every two standard 164-byte frames sent, a third 164-byte FEC packet is generated (indicated by flipping the highest bit of the sequence number) containing the XOR of the two payloads. This yields an average overhead of 1.54x.

2. Sender Architecture

The sender operates as a passive forwarder with state. It immediately forwards every incoming frame from the harness to minimize processing delay. When an even-numbered frame arrives, it caches the payload. When an odd-numbered frame arrives, it performs an XOR operation against the cached payload and immediately fires the resulting FEC packet to the relay.

3. Receiver & Jitter Buffer

The receiver utilizes a pre-allocated 100,000-element array as a jitter buffer to safely absorb reordered and duplicated packets using O(1) sequence-based indexing. We use the poll() system call to efficiently wait for network data while maintaining strict timing constraints.

4. Playout Logic

Instead of delaying playout until the absolute deadline (which caused 100% deadline misses due to microsecond OS scheduling inaccuracies), the playout loop checks the buffer. If the next required frame is available, it is dispatched to the harness player immediately. If the deadline passes and the frame (or its FEC recovery data) has not arrived, the receiver deliberately swallows the loss and increments the playout sequence to prevent cascading stalls.
